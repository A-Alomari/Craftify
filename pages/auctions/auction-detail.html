About This Item

This exquisite piece was crafted using traditional wood-firing techniques in a communal kiln on the outskirts of Madrid. The irregular patterns of the glaze are a direct result of the varying temperatures and wood ash during the 72-hour firing process, making it a truly one-of-a-kind artifact.

The form is inspired by 18th-century agrarian vessels, balanced with a modern, minimalist silhouette that emphasizes the tactile nature of the unrefined clay. Every thumbprint and subtle asymmetry tells the story of its creation—a dialogue between the artisan's intention and the unpredictable nature of fire.
